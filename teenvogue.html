TEEN VOGUE EDITOR IS OUT AFTER RACIST TWEET CONTROVERSY

After being embroiled in controversy over a series of racist anti-Asian and homophobic tweets from 2011, Alexi McCammond has resigned from her position as editor in chief of Teen Vogue. The split comes amid a surge of violence towards the Asian American community and mounting pressure from netizens and staff of the social justice-oriented title, who called on Condé Nast to take action.

“After speaking with Alexi this morning, we agreed that it was best to part ways, so as to not overshadow the important work happening at Teen Vogue,” read an internal e-mail sent to staff by Stan Duncan, chief people officer at Condé Nast. McCammond also issued a statement via Twitter saying her “past tweets have overshadowed the work I’ve done to highlight the people and issues that I care about” and sending well-wishes to the Teen Vogue team.
